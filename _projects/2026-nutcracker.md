1. Find: 

Given input parameters, we were asked to design a nutcracker mechanism that provided sufficient mechanical advantage to crack macadamia nuts while maintaining usability. 

2. Given: 

We were told to take into account the average force required to break a macadamia nut, as well as a person's maximum grip strength. We were allowed to assume simple geometry for the nutcracker. 

3. Approach and Diagram: 

I found the average force required to break a macadamia nut to be 225 kg (source: https://doi.org/10.1007/s10071-007-0131-2). The average size of a macadamia nut is 15 mm (source: https://alohafarmshawaiicom.mystagingwebsite.com/services/macadamia-nuts/). I found average grip strength to be between 44 and 52 kg for middle-aged men, and between 22 and 29 kg for middle-aged women, with these numbers decreasing with age (source: https://www.racmn.com/blog/get-a-grip-your-health-is-in-your-hands-really). For this reason, I chose to design a nutcracker that only required 20 kg of input force, for accessibility reasons. I also took into account hand span so the user could reach around the nutcracker. Because average hand span is 19 and 21 cm for women, and 21 and 24 cm for men (source: https://www.statcrunch.com/reports/view?reportid=5077&groupid=#:~:text=For%20the%20hand%20span%20of,to%20be%20a%20potential%20outlier.), I made sure the maximum distance between my nutcracker handles did not exceed 18 cm. 

Given these numbers, I calculated a necessary mechanical advantage of 225 kg / 20 kg = 11.25. I made the distance from the center of the macadamia nut to the pivot point 2 cm to ensure the nutcracker could accomodate its diameter. Then, the point of application of the force was assumed to be 22.5 cm away from the pivot point, leaving me with the following dimensions:

I found that the distance between the handles for this design with an average macadamia nut inside would be 16.875 cm, within the hand span of most people. 

4. Usability 

The biggest downside to this design is its size. My nutcracker's 22.5 cm length might be found unwieldy by some people. However, the mechanical advantage allows most people to crack most macadamia nuts, regardless of gender, age, or hand size. I think the large size is worth the increased accessibility of this design.
